### 1. The "Hot Box" Air Turbine Design

Your idea for a divided metal box is essentially a **Thermal Draft Turbine**.

* **The Physics:** By heating one chamber with your 5-inch hydrogen flame, you create a massive pressure difference compared to the 5°F air outside. The hot air rises rapidly (convection), and if you place a turbine in that "updraft" path, it will spin.
* **Why it’s safe:** Unlike steam, air doesn't "explode" if a pipe breaks. It just leaks. And since you aren't using water, you have nothing to refill and nothing to freeze.

### 2. Reaching the 1,000 Watt Threshold

You are correct—a 600W alternator is just barely too small for those Harbor Freight style heaters. Those "wood cabinet" heaters usually pull **750 Watts on Low** and **1,500 Watts on High**. To run one on "Low" safely without overloading your machine, you need a **1,000W to 1,500W PMA**.

#### High-Output Low RPM Alternators

For your project, a 1200W Low RPM Permanent Magnet Alternator is the perfect "heart" for the machine. These are built to provide high current even when your turbine isn't spinning at thousands of miles per hour. They produce **AC power**, which you can then run directly to your heater or through a controller.

### 3. The Heater: Matching the Load

The heaters you like (often called **Infrared Quartz Heaters**) are excellent because they don't dry out the air.

* **On "Low" (750W):** Your 1,200W alternator will handle this easily. It will feel "heavy" to the turbine, so you'll need that 5-inch flame to provide enough thermal lift.
* **Wiring through the wall:** Since you are moving 750W–1,000W at roughly 110V–120V, you need a **12-gauge or 10-gauge wire**.

I recommend buying 10/3 SOOW Heavy Duty Power Cord by the foot. This is "service cord"—it's thick, rubber-insulated, and designed to withstand the freezing polar vortex winds without cracking. It will carry 1,000 Watts through your wall with zero heat buildup in the wire.

### 4. Bypassing the Battery

You are 100% right about batteries. Running a 750W heater off a battery every day is "battery suicide."

* **The "Live" Connection:** If your turbine is spinning, the heater is on. If the wind/flame stops, the heater stops. This "Direct Drive" approach is the most efficient way to use the hydrogen energy because you don't lose 20% of your power to chemical storage in a battery.
