# hydrogen_powered_thermal_draft_turbine_006.md

This system is arguably the most **mechanically "honest"** way to heat a home off-grid. You aren't fighting chemistry (batteries) or friction (pistons); you are simply using **fluid dynamics** and **magnetism**.

For about **$1,000**, you are building a personal power station that uses the winter itself to stay efficient.

### 1. The Bearing Question: Ceramic vs. Steel

Skateboard "ceramic" bearings are usually **hybrid** (ceramic balls in steel races).

* **The Problem:** The steel race will still expand and contract, and the grease inside will bake at 400°F.
* **The Solution:** You want **Full Ceramic Bearings** (Zirconia or Silicon Nitride) or **Shielded High-Temp Steel Bearings**. Since you are doing a "water wheel" design, the shaft is long enough that the bearings can sit on the **outside** of the hot box, staying much cooler than the air inside.

### 2. Why "Dirty" 3-Phase AC?

The **1600W PMA** outputs 3-phase AC because it’s the most efficient way to pack copper into a circle.

* **The "Magic" of the Bridge Rectifier:** It takes those three "messy" wires and turns them into **two clean wires** (Positive and Negative DC).
* **Efficiency:** You aren't "losing" much power here. In fact, running high-wattage DC into the house is safer for your electronics than "wild" AC that changes frequency when the wind fluctuates.

### 3. Constructing the "Hot Box" without Metal Skills

You don't need to be a welder. You can use **Mechanical Fasteners**:

* **The Walls:** Use **Galvanized Steel Sheet Metal** (available at big-box hardware stores). You can cut it with tin snips.
* **The Frame:** Use **Steel Angle Iron** with pre-drilled holes. You can bolt the sheet metal to the frame using **stainless steel bolts** and **high-temp red RTV silicone** to seal the gaps.
* **The Fins:** Use **Aluminum Flashing**. It's paper-thin, light, and transfers heat instantly.

### 4. The 200W vs. 1500W DC Heater Problem

You won't find many "1500W DC heaters" because of the **Amperage** issue we discussed (it would melt standard wires).

* **Your Strategy:** Use a "Bank" of smaller DC heaters.
* **The Setup:** You can run **four 200W DC heaters** scattered around the kitchen. This is actually **better** than one big heater because it spreads the heat more evenly and doesn't pull too many Amps through a single wire.

### Recommended Shopping List for the "$1,000 Round Trip"

The Freedom II 1600 Watt PMG is the most expensive part (roughly $350–$400), but it’s the only part that *must* be professional grade. It will last 20+ years.

To turn that power into heat, these 12V 200W PTC Ceramic Heaters are your best bet. Buy four of them. They are small, safe, and can be wired in parallel.

You will need a 150A 3-Phase Bridge Rectifier to convert the PMA's output. This one is "over-built" so it will never overheat, even if you're pushing the full 1600 Watts.
